Science Behind Thagomizer

Global CLiPP-Seq

A Biochemical Technique for detecting RNA Binding Protein Occupancy Transcriptome-Wide

To identify RNA cis-regulatory elements in an unbiased manner, Ansel Lab {Link: https://ansel.ucsf.edu} has developed GCLiPP. Using GCLiPP, we identified RBP binding sites in mRNAs of human Jurkat T cells and mouse primary T cells. GCLiPP sequence tags correspond to known RBP binding sites, specifically correlating to abundant cytosolic RBPs. Thagomizer was designed to host these datasets in a format easily accessible to the research community.

HITS-CLiP

High-Throughput Sequencing of RNA

Isolated by Crosslinking Immunoprecipitation

Licatalosi et al., 2008 presents HITS- CLiPP as a powerful biochemical technique to interrogate the binding activity of specific RNA Binding Proteins. HITS-CLiPP generates a transcriptome-wide map of Protein-RNA binding sites, thus allowing for the elucidation of cis-regulatory elements.
